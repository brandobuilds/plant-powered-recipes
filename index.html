1Smoky Black Bean Soup~19g▸
A thick, smoky, deeply satisfying soup from two cans of beans and a few spices. Top-tier lazy cooking.
Prep
2 min
Cook
18 min
Protein
~19g
per serving
Serves
3
Tools:One potFork or masher
🧪
Why it works
Don't drain the cans — the starchy bean liquid is a natural thickener. Mashing half the beans gives creamy body with whole bean texture.
Ingredients
  • 2 cans (400g each) black beans — do NOT drain
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Avocado + chili flakes for topping
Steps
  1. 1Add beans (with all liquid), water, cumin, smoked paprika, garlic powder, salt to a pot. Stir.
  2. 2Boil, then simmer 15 minutes.
  3. 3Mash about half the beans in the pot with a fork.
  4. 4Stir in lime juice. Top with avocado and chili flakes.
2Golden Chickpea Tostadas~17g▸
Crunchy, golden chickpeas piled on crispy tostadas with lime and chili. Five ingredients, zero skill, maximum satisfaction.
Prep
2 min
Cook
8 min
Protein
~17g
per serving
Serves
3
Tools:Non-stick panFork
🧪
Why it works
Pressing chickpeas flat maximizes surface area (smash burger principle). More surface = more golden, crispy edges with creamy centers.
Ingredients
  • 2 cans (400g each) chickpeas, drained
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp chili flakes
  • Juice of ½ lime
  • 6 tostada shells
Steps
  1. 1Press each chickpea flat with the back of a fork.
  2. 2Heat olive oil over medium-high. Add chickpeas in a single layer. Don't touch for 3–4 min until golden.
  3. 3Flip, cook 2–3 more min. Add salt, garlic powder, chili flakes. Squeeze lime over top.
  4. 4Pile chickpeas onto tostada shells.
3Creamy Coconut Dal~19g▸
Silky, warming, and aromatic — this one-pot dal is the ultimate comfort food with almost zero active cooking.
Prep
2 min
Cook
15 min
Protein
~19g
per serving
Serves
3
Tools:One pot
🧪
Why it works
Red lentils need no soaking and collapse into a creamy porridge in 15 minutes. Coconut milk is a built-in thickener — no cream, no roux, no effort.
Ingredients
  • 1 cup (200g) red lentils, rinsed
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 1½ cups water
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Juice of ½ lime
  • Tortillas for serving
Steps
  1. 1Heat olive oil in a pot over medium heat. Add garlic powder, stir 15 seconds.
  2. 2Add lentils, coconut milk, water, turmeric, cumin, salt. Stir once.
  3. 3Bring to a boil, reduce to low. Simmer 15 minutes until lentils are soft and broken down.
  4. 4Stir in lime juice. Serve with warmed tortillas.
4Crispy Sweet Potato Tacos~18g▸
Caramelized sweet potato cubes and seasoned black beans piled into warm tortillas with avocado and salsa. Weeknight perfection.
Prep
5 min
Cook
15 min
Protein
~18g
per serving
Serves
3
Tools:Large non-stick pan
🧪
Why it works
Sweet potatoes caramelize fast when cut small and left undisturbed — the natural sugars brown and crisp the edges. Smoked paprika amplifies the sweetness into something savory.
Ingredients
  • 1 large sweet potato, cut into small cubes
  • 1 can (400g) black beans, drained
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 3 flour tortillas
  • Avocado, salsa, + lime for topping
Steps
  1. 1Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium-high. Add sweet potato cubes in a single layer — don't touch for 4 minutes.
  2. 2Flip, cook another 4 minutes until golden and tender. Add smoked paprika, cumin, garlic powder, salt. Toss to coat.
  3. 3Push sweet potato to one side. Add black beans to the empty side, cook 2–3 minutes until heated through.
  4. 4Warm tortillas (dry pan 15 sec per side or microwave). Load with sweet potato, beans, avocado, salsa, and a squeeze of lime.
5Spiced Walnut Burritos~21g▸
Smoky, savory bean-walnut filling wrapped in warm tortillas. Nobody will ask where the meat is.
Prep
5 min
Cook
10 min
Protein
~21g
per serving
Serves
3
Tools:Large panFork
🧪
Why it works
Mashed black beans + chopped walnuts = a ground-meat texture and color. Soy sauce and smoked paprika create deep, savory flavor that mimics taco meat.
Ingredients
  • 1 can (400g) black beans, drained
  • ½ cup (50g) walnuts, chopped
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • 3 flour tortillas
  • Salsa + avocado for topping
Steps
  1. 1Add black beans to a pan over medium heat. Mash roughly with a fork — leave some whole.
  2. 2Stir in walnuts, soy sauce, smoked paprika, cumin, garlic powder. Cook 5–7 min until thick.
  3. 3Warm tortillas (microwave 15 sec or dry pan).
  4. 4Fill tortillas, add salsa and avocado, wrap, eat.
6 Salt & Pepper Tofu ~20g ▸
Shatteringly crispy tofu bites tossed with garlic, black pepper, and chili flakes. Addictive enough to eat straight from the pan.
Prep
5 min
Cook
10 min
Protein
~20g
per serving
Serves
3
Tools:Large non-stick panMixing bowl
🧪
Why it works
Tearing tofu by hand creates irregular edges with more surface area = more crispy bits. Cornstarch coating crisps up in oil without deep-frying.
Ingredients
  • 1 block (350g) extra-firm tofu
  • 3 tbsp cornstarch
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp chili flakes
  • 3 scallions, sliced
Steps
  1. 1Drain the tofu and tear it into bite-sized chunks by hand (irregular edges = more crispy bits).
  2. 2Toss in a bowl with cornstarch, garlic powder, black pepper, and salt until evenly coated.
  3. 3Heat olive oil in a large non-stick pan over medium-high. Add tofu in a single layer — don't touch for 4 minutes.
  4. 4Flip and cook another 4 minutes until golden and crispy.
  5. 5Toss with chili flakes and scallions. Serve over rice or eat on its own.
7Creamy Peanut Ramen~24g▸
A creamy, spicy peanut broth with noodles and crispy tofu. Restaurant-level ramen from pantry staples.
Prep
5 min
Cook
15 min
Protein
~24g
per serving
Serves
3
Tools:Large potSmall panBowl
🧪
Why it works
Peanut butter emulsifies directly into hot water to create a rich, creamy broth instantly — no slow-simmered stock needed.
Ingredients
  • 200g spaghetti, broken in half
  • 1 block (350g) extra-firm tofu, torn
  • 3 tbsp peanut butter
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • ½ tsp chili flakes
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 handfuls frozen spinach
  • 2 scallions, sliced
Steps
  1. 1Whisk peanut butter, soy sauce, chili flakes, and garlic powder in a bowl. Set aside.
  2. 2Heat olive oil in a small pan. Add torn tofu, cook 3–4 min per side until browned. Set aside.
  3. 3Boil 4 cups water in a large pot. Add broken spaghetti, cook 8 minutes. Don't drain.
  4. 4Stir peanut butter mixture into the pot. Add frozen spinach, stir until heated (2 min).
  5. 5Divide into bowls. Top with crispy tofu and scallions.
8Rich Lentil Bolognese~22g▸
A rich, savory pasta sauce where red lentils melt away into nothing — you'd never know it's vegan. One pot, no chopping.
Prep
5 min
Cook
25 min
Protein
~22g
per serving
Serves
3
Tools:One large potOne pot for pasta
🧪
Why it works
Red lentils completely dissolve when simmered, becoming a thick, meaty sauce with zero effort. Soy sauce adds umami glutamates that trick your brain into tasting meat.
Ingredients
  • 1 cup (200g) red lentils, rinsed
  • 1 can (400g) diced tomatoes
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp maple syrup
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 300g spaghetti
Steps
  1. 1Add lentils, diced tomatoes, soy sauce, water, garlic powder, maple syrup, and salt to a large pot. Stir.
  2. 2Bring to a boil, then reduce to a low simmer. Cook 20–25 minutes, stirring every few minutes, until lentils dissolve.
  3. 3Cook spaghetti in a separate pot per package directions. Drain.
  4. 4Taste sauce — add more salt or soy sauce if needed. Serve over pasta.
9Kenji's Chickpea Stew~18g▸
A Spanish-inspired stew that simmers down into something deeply rich and savory. Low effort, high reward — just let the pot do the work.
Prep
10 min
Cook
40 min
Protein
~18g
per serving
Serves
3
Tools:BlenderOne large pot
🧪
Why it works
Blending half the tomatoes creates a smooth base while the crushed chunks give texture. Simmering uncovered concentrates flavors — and the lime + finishing olive oil at the end is a restaurant trick that lifts everything.
Ingredients
  • 3 tbsp olive oil (+ 1 tbsp for finishing)
  • 1½ tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 can (400g) diced tomatoes
  • 2 cans (400g each) chickpeas — do NOT drain
  • 3 cups frozen spinach
  • ½ tsp salt
  • Juice of ½ lime (to finish)
  • Tortillas for serving
Steps
  1. 1Blend half the can of diced tomatoes (with juice) until smooth. Leave the other half chunky.
  2. 2Heat 3 tbsp olive oil in a pot over medium. Add garlic powder and smoked paprika — stir 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. 3Pour in the blended tomato mixture. Bring to a gentle simmer.
  4. 4Add the remaining chunky tomatoes, frozen spinach, and both cans of chickpeas with all their liquid. Add salt. Stir.
  5. 5Simmer uncovered 30–40 minutes, stirring occasionally, until thick and stew-like.
  6. 6Finish with lime juice and 1 tbsp olive oil. Stir, taste, adjust salt. Serve with warmed tortillas.
10Chocolate Peanut Mousse~14g▸
A decadent, silky mousse that's secretly packed with protein. Dump everything in a blender, chill, and impress.
Prep
5 min + chill
Cook
None
Protein
~14g
per serving
Serves
3
Tools:Blender or food processor
🧪
Why it works
Canned chickpeas blend smooth because their starches emulsify like cream. You'd never know they're in there — all you taste is chocolate and peanut butter.
Ingredients
  • 1 can (400g) chickpeas, drained
  • 3 tbsp peanut butter
  • 3 tbsp cocoa powder
  • 3 tbsp maple syrup
  • ¼ cup coconut milk
  • Pinch of salt
Steps
  1. 1Add chickpeas, peanut butter, cocoa, maple syrup, coconut milk, and salt to a blender.
  2. 2Blend on high 2–3 minutes until smooth. Scrape sides halfway.
  3. 3Taste — add more syrup or cocoa as you like.
  4. 4Divide into 3 cups. Chill at least 30 minutes. Top with whatever you like.
